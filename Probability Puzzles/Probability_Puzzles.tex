\section{Symmetry \& Total Probability}
\begin{question}
Two gamblers A and B have $n+1$ and $n$ fair coins respectively. If both toss
all their coins, find the probability that the number of heads of A is greater than the
number of heads of B.
\end{question}
\begin{solution}
Define events: \\
$E$: $A$ has more heads in the first $n$ tosses \\
$F$: $B$ has more heads in the first $n$ tosses \\
$G$: $A$ and $B$ have equal heads in the first $n$ tosses \\
$H$: $A$ gets a head in $(n+1)^{th}$ toss

By law of total probability, $P(E) + P(F) + P(G) = 1$. By symmetry, $P(E) = P(F)$, so:
\begin{equation}
2P(E) + P(G) = 1 \label{eq:total_prob}
\end{equation}

We require the probability that $A$ has more heads than $B$ after $n+1$ tosses. This occurs if $E$ happens (regardless of $H$) OR if $G$ happens AND $H$ happens:
\[ P(\text{Result}) = P(E) + P(G)P(H) \]

Since $P(H) = \frac{1}{2}$, we have:
\[ P(\text{Result}) = P(E) + \frac{1}{2}P(G) = \frac{2P(E) + P(G)}{2} \]

Substituting from (\ref{eq:total_prob}), we get $P(\text{Result}) = \frac{1}{2}$
\end{solution}

\begin{question}
A, B and C toss a fair coin and get their first heads in $N_A$, $N_B$ and $N_C$ tosses respectively.
Find $P(N_A < N_B < N_C)$.
\end{question}
\begin{solution}
Let D be the event that $N_A \neq N_B \neq N_C$. By symmetry, each of the 6 permutations under the
event D are equally likely:
\[P(N_A < N_B < N_C) = \frac{1}{6} \times P(D)\]
\begin{equation}
  P(D) = 1 - 3 \times P(two\:are\:same) - P(three\:are\:same)\label{eq:total_prob}
\end{equation}
$N_A$, $N_B$ and $N_C$ follow the geometric distribution, $(1/2)^n$ where n=1,2... so,
\[P(two\:are\:same) = \sum_{k = 1}^{n}{(\frac{1}{2})^{2n}(1 - (\frac{1}{2})^n)} = 
\sum_{k = 1}^{n}{(\frac{1}{2})^{2n}} - \sum_{k = 1}^{n}{(\frac{1}{2})^{3n}}
= \frac{1}{3} - \frac{1}{7} = \frac{4}{21} \text{ and }
P(three\:are\:same) = \sum_{k = 1}^{n}{(\frac{1}{2})^{3n}} = \frac{1}{7}\]
Substituting the above values in (2),
\[
P(D) = 1 - 3 \times \frac{4}{21} - \frac{1}{7} = \frac{2}{7} \text{ hence, }
P(N_A < N_B < N_C) = \frac{1}{6} \times \frac{2}{7} = \frac{1}{21}
\]
\end{solution}
